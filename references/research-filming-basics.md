# Filming Basics Research -- For Handbook Ch.05

## Camera Settings

- **Resolution**: 4K if possible (reframe headroom), 1080p fine if storage limited
- **Frame rate**: 30fps standard. 24fps for cinematic. 60fps only for slow-mo segments.
- **Shutter speed**: Double frame rate (180-degree rule). 30fps → 1/60. 60fps → 1/125.
- **White balance**: MANUAL. 5600K for daylight/LEDs, 3200K for tungsten. Use gray card.

## Lighting Setups

**Talking head**: Key light at 45 degrees + fill/bounce opposite side.
**Overhead workbench**: Two matched LED panels at equal angles, 45 degrees down. Eliminates hand shadows.
**Detail closeups**: Single LED on-axis for even light, or single side light at raking angle for texture.

## Budget Gear

- Neewer 660 LED panels (~$40 each) best value
- White foam board ($5) as bounce reflector
- Window light works but inconsistent (supplement with LED fill)

## VFR Prevention

Most phones default to VFR. Force CFR with:
- **iOS**: Filmic Pro
- **Android**: Open Camera
- GoPros: check mode settings
- Always verify with MediaInfo before editing

## Mic Recommendations

- **Talking to camera**: Lapel/lav (Rode Wireless Go II, ~$150)
- **Workshop ambient**: Shotgun on-camera (Rode VideoMicro, ~$60)
- **Voiceover in post**: USB condenser (AT2020, ~$100)

## Audio Levels

Set gain so peaks hit **-12dB**. Never exceed **-6dB**. Record test with loudest expected sound before every session.

## Multi-Device Sync

Clap once on camera at start of every take. Align audio spike in post.

## Common Mistakes

Backlighting from windows behind you. Cluttered backgrounds. No tripod. Inconsistent framing between cuts (mark tripod position with tape). Filming and narrating simultaneously (voiceover in post is easier + better).

## Minimum Viable Setup (Priority Order, ~$100-120)

1. **Tripod** ($25) -- eliminates biggest quality problem
2. **Lav mic** ($30-60 wired) -- audio > video quality
3. **One LED panel** ($40) -- consistent light
4. **Phone with CFR app** or existing DSLR
5. **White foam board** ($5) -- bounce fill

Upgrade order: stability → audio → lighting → camera last.
