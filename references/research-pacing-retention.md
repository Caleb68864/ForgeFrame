# Pacing & Retention Research -- For Handbook Ch.11

## Retention Data

- 20% leave in first 15 seconds. Decision to stay happens in ~8 seconds.
- Tutorial content averages 42% retention (strong niche). 40-60% is good for 10-20 min.
- Biggest drop-off at 1-minute mark. Secondary dip around minute 8.
- "Good abandonment": YouTube doesn't penalize tutorial viewers leaving after finding their answer.

## Speaking Pace

- Target: **140-160 WPM** for general narration
- Slow to **110-130 WPM** for complex steps
- Never exceed **180 WPM**
- Vary pace deliberately -- monotone kills retention

## Visual Change Frequency

- Talking head: cut every **15-25 seconds**
- Process montage: **3-8 second** shots
- Engaging close-ups: can hold **up to 40 seconds**
- If nothing new in 5 seconds, viewer's eye wanders

## Energy Curve (10-20 min tutorial)

| Segment | Time | Energy | Style |
|---------|------|--------|-------|
| Hook | 0:00-0:30 | HIGH | Fast cuts, payoff tease |
| Setup | 0:30-3:00 | Medium-high | Visual resets every 10-20s |
| Core teaching | 3:00-12:00 | Steady medium | 25-40s spacing, b-roll heavy |
| Energy bump | Every 2-3 min | Brief spike | Pattern interrupt |
| Climax | Last 3-4 min | Rising | Tighter cuts, music builds |
| Payoff | Final 30-60s | HIGH | Money shot, slow pan |

## First 30 Seconds Must Do Three Things

1. Show the finished result (the promise)
2. State who it's for and what they'll learn (one sentence)
3. Pattern interrupt at 25-35 seconds (angle change, music drop)

Skip branding, lengthy intros, "hey guys."

## B-Roll as Pacing Tool

- 40-70% of tutorial runtime should be b-roll
- Cut at natural sentence breaks, never mid-thought
- Let b-roll breathe 1-2 seconds past voiceover

## Chapter Markers

- +25% watch time on tutorials
- Convert bounces into skips (viewer jumps to section, stays)
- Titles indexed by search engines

## Pro Creator Patterns

- **Adam Savage**: Opens with finished piece. "One day build" = time pressure tension. Process b-roll + voiceover reflection. Talks through mistakes.
- **Jimmy Diresta**: Near-wordless, music-driven. Speed contrast IS the energy curve.
- **Laura Kampf**: Visual-first + personality cuts. Short reveals create satisfying arcs.
- **Common thread**: Front-load payoff, process carries middle, reveal at end.

## Common Pacing Mistakes

1. 30+ second intro before showing project
2. Explaining same concept twice without new visuals
3. Skipping glamour shot of finished piece
4. Flat energy throughout (lecture feel)
5. Reading tool/material lists (put on screen instead)
6. No chapter markers on 10+ min videos
