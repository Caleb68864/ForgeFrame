# Audio Production Research -- For Handbook Ch.10

## YouTube Loudness Standards

- Target: **-14 LUFS** integrated loudness
- True peak limit: **-1 dBTP**
- YouTube turns loud audio down (sounds worse). Quiet audio stays quiet (not boosted).

## Voice Signal Chain (Order Matters)

1. **Noise reduction** -- remove hiss/hum first
2. **EQ** -- shape voice tone
3. **Compression** -- even out dynamics
4. **Limiter** -- catch peaks, ceiling at -1 dBTP
5. **Normalization** -- bring to -14 LUFS

## Noise Floor

- Acceptable: **-60 dBFS or lower**
- Above -50 dBFS becomes distracting
- Fix: turn off HVAC, get mic closer, noise gate or spectral denoising

## Compression (Plain English)

Makes quiet words louder and loud words quieter. For voiceover: **3:1 to 4:1 ratio**, attack 5-10ms, release 50-100ms. Target 3-6 dB gain reduction on peaks.

## EQ for Voice Clarity

- **High-pass at 80-100 Hz** -- removes rumble
- **Cut 200-400 Hz** by 2-3 dB -- reduces mud/boxiness
- **Boost 2-5 kHz** by 1-3 dB -- presence/clarity
- **Gentle boost 8-12 kHz** -- "air" if voice sounds dull
- Cut before you boost. Less is more.

## Room Treatment (Budget)

Moving blankets on walls. Thick rug on hard floors. Bookshelves = excellent diffusers. Closets = vocal booths. Skip acoustic foam (only absorbs highs, leaves boxy low-mids).

## Microphone Placement

- **Lapel/lav**: 6-8 inches below chin, centered
- **USB condenser**: 6-10 inches, slightly off-axis, pop filter
- **Shotgun/boom**: 2-3 feet, overhead just out of frame

## Common Mistakes

Inconsistent levels between segments. Room echo. Mouth clicks (stay hydrated). Background fans/fridges. Clipping from gain too high. Music competing with voice.

## Before/After

**Before**: thin, distant, hissy, peaks that make you flinch, rumble, inconsistent volume.
**After**: close, warm, even, silent background, every word equally audible, controlled sibilance.

## Monitoring

Record with closed-back headphones. Edit with headphones. Never trust laptop speakers.

## #1 Tip

Get the mic closer to your mouth. Improves SNR more than any plugin.
